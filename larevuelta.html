What this is
La REVUELTA is an independent, bilingual documentary series produced through For The Culture NY.

Each issue focuses on one city and one theme.

Through field documentation, community conversations, and photography, each edition is developed in collaboration with local people and organizations. Published in English and Spanish, the work is returned to the communities it comes from.
From the field
Resistance can take forms in so many ways — through activism, but also through culture, food, art, community, and simply life itself.

While we live in a world filled with violence and fear, it's also important to pause, to see the beauty, and to hear the voices of communities that have come together and chosen to speak out — in both obvious and not so obvious ways.

La REVUELTA documents those voices. People, communities, art, subcultures, and food that have chosen to participate in this movement in unique ways — as well as personal reflections from the field.

So far: Guatemala and Mexico. More coming.

Approach / Methodology
The work is built through a field-based, relational process:
Go first
Before any formal documentation, move through a place — make connections, learn in context what is alive and what matters. The theme emerges from that.
Collaborate
Every issue is made with a local collaborator — a person or organization embedded in the community. Their knowledge and relationships are what make the documentation accountable.
Document
Two rounds of conversations — one for understanding, one for documentation. Field observation, photography, desk research. Everything in Spanish.
Return it
The zine is bilingual and made for the communities in it. Not for outside audiences. The people who made it possible receive the output.
Field notes from Xela, Guatemala
Live documentation from the field — posted on @fortheculture_ny as the work happens.
Xela, Guatemala March 2025
Resistencia, Xela
Underground scenes in Xela: live music, political tables, art, people asking hard questions about what resistance actually looks like.
Xela, Guatemala March 2025
Women's March, Xela
Was lucky to be in Xela on International Women's Day — March 8. Walked next to women and young girls from the center to the intercultural center. Signs reading "Es mi primera marcha." "Por las que lucharon y no fueron escuchadas."
Xela, Guatemala March 2025
Trama Textil
Met incredible women and their craft. So much skill, patience, and care in every step of the process — something really beautiful about sitting together, weaving side by side. Culture being kept alive, quietly, intentionally, as resistance.
San Cristóbal, Chiapas, Mexico March 2025
Street Art, San Cristóbal
Walls covered in murals and political graphics — a city that wears its voice publicly.
Oaxaca, Mexico March 2025
Grabado, Oaxaca
Printmaking is one of the most popular forms of art in Oaxaca City — you can see it everywhere. A huge connection between art and political resistance. More popular spaces exist in the center; smaller, more underground spaces exist for those who know where to look.
Oaxaca, Mexico March 2025
Feminism y Grabado, Oaxaca
Took a class on Social Movements through a local org and was connected with feminist printmakers in Oaxaca. Women here are doing powerful work — the kind that doesn't always show up in obvious ways.
